## Adding Your Own Patterns

As you write more docs, look for moments where a sentence or paragraph lands particularly well. Ask yourself:

1. **What pattern is this?** Give it a name -- "Stakes before solutions," "Concede to strengthen," "Numbers as proof."
2. **Why does it work?** Not just "it sounds good" but what structural or rhetorical move makes it effective?
3. **What's the anti-pattern?** Showing the bad version helps Claude (and you) recognize the failure mode.

Good categories to build over time:

- **Opening moves** -- how you start sections, how you frame problems
- **Evidence patterns** -- how you use numbers, quotes, examples
- **Decision language** -- how you recommend, how you handle uncertainty
- **Audience shifts** -- how your voice changes for internal vs. external, exec vs. eng
- **Structural moves** -- how you use tables, lists, headers for clarity

The goal isn't completeness. Five strong examples teach more than fifty mediocre ones.
